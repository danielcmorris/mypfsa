Frequently Asked Questions

You Can Learn a Lot From Our Questions Answer

  • A life insurance policy is a contract that pays a tax-free benefit to your loved ones when you pass away. It provides financial security to help cover living expenses, debts, and long-term family needs.

  • An Endowment Contract that allows you to pass on your legacy without penalties for your heirs

  • An annuity is a financial contract that turns your savings into a predictable stream of income, often for the rest of your life. It is designed to provide stability, tax-deferred growth, and protection against outliving your money.

  • A standard IRA is for individuals to save for retirement using their own earned income, while a SEP IRA is designed for self-employed individuals and small-business owners to make much larger, tax-deductible employer contributions. The SEP IRA allows significantly higher annual contributions, making it a powerful tool for members who want to accelerate retirement savings.

  • A Coverdell Education Savings Account (ESA) is a tax-advantaged account that lets families save and invest for a child’s education, with earnings growing tax-free when used for qualified expenses. It can be used for both K–12 and college costs, including tuition, books, tutoring, and even computers

  • PFSA members graduating from High school throughout the United States who meet the eligibility requirements may apply for four–year or two-year scholarships.
