Foundation FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our scholarship and grant programs.

Who is eligible for PFSA scholarships?

Applicants must be PFSA members for two years prior to the application deadline, maintain a minimum 3.0 GPA for four-year institutions (2.5 for two-year/vocational), and be high school graduates entering accredited institutions as full-time students.

When is the scholarship application deadline?

The scholarship application deadline is March 1 each year. Applications must be submitted by email to Pfsa.scholarships@gmail.com. Faxed applications are not accepted.

Can non-members apply for scholarships?

The Joseph Fagundes Memorial Scholarship is open to non-members who are Humboldt County, California residents. All other scholarships require PFSA membership for at least two years.

What is the difference between scholarships and grants?

Scholarships have GPA requirements and a March 1 deadline. Education grants have no minimum GPA requirement, accept applications year-round, and are reviewed four times annually. Grants are one-time only.

Do I need to demonstrate financial need?

No, demonstrating financial need is not required for either scholarships or education grants. The programs focus on academic achievement and commitment to education.

Can I apply for both a scholarship and a grant?

Applicants who have received a PFSA Grant or have an open PFSA Scholarship are not eligible to apply for the Education Grant Program. Grants are one-time awards.

Are vocational and trade schools eligible?

Yes! Both scholarship and grant programs support students attending accredited vocational and trade schools. The minimum GPA for two-year/vocational programs is 2.5 for scholarships, while grants have no GPA requirement.

How can I support the foundation?

You can contribute through In Honor or In Memory donations, establish annual named scholarships/grants (minimum $500), or include the foundation in your estate planning through cash bequests. All donations are tax-deductible.
